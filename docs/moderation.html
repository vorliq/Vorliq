Community Identity and Moderation

Vorliq moderation is designed to reduce spam, impersonation, abuse, and scams without KYC and without rewriting blockchain history.

Wallet Verification

Wallet verification proves only that someone can sign a short challenge for a Vorliq wallet address. It does not verify a real-world name, legal identity, age, location, government ID, or organization.

The private key stays in the user's browser, mobile device, or SDK caller environment. Vorliq stores the public verification message, timestamp, and verified status, not a private key.

Trust Labels

Labels such as Wallet Verified, Unverified Wallet, Active Contributor, Top Reputation, and New Member are factual public signals. They are not endorsements, guarantees, legal identity checks, or promises that a user is safe to trade with.

Reports

Members can report forum posts, replies, chat messages, and profiles for spam, impersonation, abuse, scams, illegal content, or other issues. Reports create a moderator review queue only; they do not automatically remove content.

Reports must not include private keys, wallet passwords, admin tokens, recovery material, IP addresses, raw user agents, or sensitive personal documents.

Moderation States

Forum content can be visible, hidden, locked, pinned, or unpinned. Hidden content is withheld from normal lists; locked posts cannot receive new replies. These actions do not delete historical forum data or alter blockchain records.

If moderation appears mistaken, use the public community channels linked from Vorliq to ask for review. Include the post, reply, chat message, or profile identifier, but never include private keys or passwords.

What Is Never Collected

Vorliq moderation does not require KYC, government ID, legal-name verification, private keys, wallet passwords, admin tokens, IP-address publication, raw user-agent publication, or server path disclosure.
