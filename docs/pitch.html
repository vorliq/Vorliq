The Problem

Many communities still lack practical digital tools for transparent records, shared savings-style coordination, local support, and community decision making. Existing systems are often designed around institutions rather than the people trying to build security together.

Existing cryptocurrency projects often promise access, but they are usually too complex and too expensive for ordinary communities to use. They depend on outside networks, confusing wallets, volatile fees, and technical workflows that make simple saving, lending, and local exchange harder than they need to be.

Community savings groups already exist all over the world. People pool money, lend to one another, build trust, and create local accountability, but most groups have no digital infrastructure that reflects how they actually operate or gives them transparent records they can own.

Slide 3

The Solution

Vorliq is a self-contained blockchain platform built for community coordination, shared records, and savings-style and lending-style workflows. It combines a native coin, wallet tools, peer community requests, governance, treasury features, achievements, chat, and network diagnostics in one practical system.

Vorliq runs on its own blockchain. Communities can use wallets, local records, peer nodes, governance proposals, lending-style requests, community coordination, and treasury workflows inside one open-source system.

The result is software where communities can inspect the code, run their own nodes, and shape their own rules. VLQ is the native coin for transparent records and collective coordination, not a promise of market value.

Slide 4

How It Works

Vorliq runs a proof of work blockchain, which means nodes compete to solve a small cryptographic challenge before adding the next block of records. Each block links to the one before it, making the history difficult to rewrite and easy for nodes to verify.

VLQ is the native coin of the network, with a maximum supply of 21 million. Members can create wallets, receive mining rewards, send signed transactions, and use VLQ inside community coordination, lending-style requests, peer community requests, treasury proposals, and payment flows.

The lending system lets members request support and lets the community vote on whether those requests should be approved. The community request flow lets members coordinate requesting VLQ or offering VLQ in real-world terms such as money, goods, services, or time. Governance lets VLQ holders vote on supported network rules, while the treasury gives the community a shared pool for proposed work.

The peer to peer network lets Vorliq nodes discover each other, share transactions and blocks, synchronize chain history, and monitor node health. In simple terms, Vorliq is designed so communities can run the network together instead of depending on one company to hold the records.
