Terms of Use

Vorliq is open source community software. Users run, connect, and use Vorliq at their own responsibility. VLQ is the native coin of the Vorliq community blockchain and is not a promise of profit, a bank deposit, or a claim against any company.

Wallet Responsibility

Users are responsible for protecting their private keys. Lost private keys cannot be recovered by Vorliq, the community, or the project maintainers. Users should test small amounts first and keep backups safely.

Self-Custody

Vorliq is a self-custody blockchain application. A private key controls the wallet address connected to it. Anyone who obtains a user's private key can spend funds or act as that wallet. Vorliq does not custody user wallets, does not know wallet passwords, and cannot reverse transactions because a key was lost, stolen, copied, or entered into an unsafe website.

Users must keep encrypted wallet backups, private keys, and passwords safe. Users should verify they are using the official site at https://vorliq.org or their own trusted local node before entering sensitive wallet information.

Community Features

Lending, exchange, forum, governance, treasury, chat, and registry features are community tools. Users should behave lawfully, respectfully, and carefully when making proposals, offers, votes, posts, or trades.
